# Student guide

**Audience:** students.

You see **your own courses and your own work**, and nothing else. You never see
another student's submissions, grades, or comments — rosters show your instructors
and TAs, and classmates stay private. For the complete picture of who can see
what, see [Roles and permissions](../reference/roles-and-permissions.md).

---

## Joining a course

You join a course one of two ways:

- An **instructor enrolls you** directly, or
- They **share an enroll code** that you enter yourself. Self-enrollment works only
  while the course is published and its **enrollment window is open**, and the code
  must be correct.

A course appears to you only **after the instructor publishes it**. Before that —
or if it has been archived — it simply won't show up, even by a direct link. You
cannot remove yourself from a course; ask your instructor if you need to be
un-enrolled.

---

## Finding your work

Your **dashboard** and the **calendar** show assignments from your published
courses, bucketed by date. Each assignment lists its problems and its due date.

Deadlines appear in **both your local time and the course's timezone**
(for example, "Nov 5, 11:59 PM EST · course zone US/Eastern"), so you always know
the real cutoff even if you're travelling. Only published assignments from
published courses appear — drafts your instructor is still preparing are hidden.

---

## Submitting

Open a problem and submit your file. A few things worth knowing:

- **Submission limits.** A problem may cap how many times you can submit. When it
  does, you can see the limit, and a submission that would go past it is refused.
  Some problems allow unlimited attempts.
- **Submissions are final.** You can't edit or delete a submission — a new
  submission is a **new attempt**, and each attempt counts toward any limit.
- **Deadlines.** After the due date, work is accepted only if the assignment allows
  late submissions, and only up to the late cutoff your instructor set. Whether
  something is late is decided by the server, so changing your device clock or
  timezone won't help.
- **Resubmit cooldown.** There may be a short minimum wait between resubmissions to
  the same problem; if you submit again too quickly you'll be asked to wait a moment.

---

## Feedback and grades

When **autograding** is on, your result appears once the grader finishes. You can
view the **feedback and score for your own work**. If an instructor leaves a
**comment** on your work, you'll see it in your own thread and can reply.

You only ever see **your own** grades and feedback.

---

## Group assignments

Some assignments are done as a **group**. Your group shares **one submission**: any
member can submit on the group's behalf, and everyone in the group sees the
submission, its grade, and its feedback (and the group's comment thread). You never
see another group's work.

---

## Your profile

From your profile you can update your **name, avatar, and timezone**. Set your
timezone to **Automatic** to follow your device, or pick a fixed zone. Your
**email is your account name and cannot be changed** — contact an administrator if
it's wrong.

---

## Trouble signing in

Too many failed login attempts will **lock your account** for a while. Wait for the
lockout to pass and try again. If you're still locked out, or you've forgotten your
password, contact an administrator, who can unlock the account or reset your
password. If your password was reset for you, you'll be asked to choose a new one
when you next sign in.
