# Student guide

Audience: students. You see your own courses and your own work. You never see another student's submissions, grades, or comments.

## Joining a course

You join a course one of two ways: an instructor enrolls you, or they share an enroll code that you enter while signup is open. A course appears to you only after the instructor publishes it.

## Finding your work

Your dashboard and the calendar show assignments from your published courses. Each assignment lists its problems and its due date. Deadlines appear in both your local time and the course's timezone, so you always know the real cutoff.

## Submitting

Open a problem and submit your file. A few things worth knowing:

- A problem may limit how many times you can submit. When it does, you can see the limit, and a submission that would go past it is refused.
- Submissions are final. A new submission is a new attempt, and each attempt counts toward any limit.
- After the due date, work is accepted only if the assignment allows late submissions, and only up to any late cutoff the instructor set.

## Feedback and grades

When autograding is on, your result appears once the grader finishes. You can view the feedback and score for your own work. If an instructor comments on your work, you see it in your own thread and can reply.

## Your profile

Update your name, avatar, and timezone from your profile. Set your timezone to Automatic to follow your device. Your email is your account name and cannot be changed.

## Trouble signing in

Too many failed attempts will lock your account for a while. If you are still locked out after that, contact an administrator, who can unlock the account or reset your password.
