# Faculty and TA guide

Audience: instructors and teaching assistants. Your role is set per course, and faculty and TAs currently have the same abilities within a course. Everything here applies to the courses you are assigned to and to no others.

## Courses

- View and edit your assigned courses.
- Publish a course to make it visible to enrolled students. An unpublished course is invisible to students, by link and by search alike.
- Archive a course at the end of a term. Archiving makes it read only for everyone. Ask an administrator if you need it reopened.

Once a course is archived, its settings, dates, roster, and grades are frozen.

## Timezone and deadlines

Each course has a timezone, which defaults to the server default. Due dates you enter are read in the course timezone and stored as a single moment in time, so every student sees the same deadline no matter where they are. Students see each deadline in both their local time and the course zone.

## Assignments and problems

- Create an assignment with a due date, and optionally allow late submissions up to a cutoff.
- Add problems to an assignment. For each problem you set the points, whether autograding is on, and the maximum number of submissions a student may make.
- Upload the answer or solution file for a problem. Students never see or download these files.
- Publish an assignment to release it. Unpublished assignments do not appear to students. On your calendar they show marked as Draft so you can tell them apart.

## Roster and enrollment

- Add or remove members, and assign any course role, including making another person faculty or TA.
- Enroll a student directly at any time, or share an enroll code for self enrollment while signup is open.
- Removing a student revokes access but keeps their work. Re-enroll them and their submissions and grades reattach.

Students cannot remove themselves from a course.

## Grading and feedback

- View every student's submissions and grades in your course.
- With autograding on, submissions are scored automatically. You can override a score by hand, including your own, and every override is recorded in the audit log.
- Re-run a single submission or every submission in a course after you change a problem or its solution.
- Export grades for your courses.
- Comment on a student's work to leave feedback. Each student sees only their own thread and your replies. Students cannot delete comments; staff can.

## Password resets

You can reset the password of a student enrolled in one of your courses. You cannot act on other staff, or on anyone who is not a student in a course you teach.

## Groups

A group assignment has one shared submission per group. Any member can submit, and every member sees the submission, its grade, and its feedback. Visibility never crosses into another group.
